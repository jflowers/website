## Reading to Know Me

[Siddhartha](http://www.amazon.com/Siddhartha-Hermann-Hesse/dp/081120068X/sr=8-4/qid=1170206979/ref=pd_bbs_4/105-6244180-7165267?ie=UTF8&s=books) by Hermann Hesse

[![Siddhartha](http://ec2.images-amazon.com/images/P/081120068X.01._BO2,204,203,200_PIlitb-dp-500-arrow,TopRight,45,-64_OU01_AA240_SH20_SCLZZZZZZZ_.gif)](http://www.amazon.com/Siddhartha-Hermann-Hesse/dp/081120068X/sr=8-4/qid=1170206979/ref=pd_bbs_4/105-6244180-7165267?ie=UTF8&s=books)

This was a very quick read, it is only 122 pages.  It was nice to read a fiction, a story about someone struggle to awaken.

[Do You Do It, or Does It Do You?: how to let the universe meditate you](http://www.amazon.com/Do-You-Does-universe-meditate/dp/1591793572/sr=1-6/qid=1170207477/ref=sr_1_6/105-6244180-7165267?ie=UTF8&s=books) by Alan Watts

[![Do You Do It, or Does It Do You?: how to let the universe meditate you](http://ec1.images-amazon.com/images/P/1591793572.01._AA240_SCLZZZZZZZ_.jpg)](http://www.amazon.com/Do-You-Does-universe-meditate/dp/1591793572/sr=1-6/qid=1170207477/ref=sr_1_6/105-6244180-7165267?ie=UTF8&s=books) 

This was my most recent Alan Watts read.  It was a good book.  It was mostly a re-hashing of things that I have heard him say in other works.  This is a good book if you are new to Alan, other wise I would not spend the time on it.

[The Way of Zen](http://www.amazon.com/Way-Zen-Alan-W-Watts/dp/0375705104/sr=1-22/qid=1170207679/ref=sr_1_22/105-6244180-7165267?ie=UTF8&s=books) by Alan W. Watts

[![The Way of Zen](http://ec2.images-amazon.com/images/P/0375705104.01._BO2,204,203,200_PIsitb-dp-500-arrow,TopRight,45,-64_OU01_AA240_SH20_SCLZZZZZZZ_.gif)](http://www.amazon.com/Way-Zen-Alan-W-Watts/dp/0375705104/sr=1-22/qid=1170207679/ref=sr_1_22/105-6244180-7165267?ie=UTF8&s=books)

I greatly enjoyed this book.  In fact I re-read the last half of it like 5 times.  It really helped me to become aware of and see nondual, as well as to cease much of my grasping.

[The Book: On the Taboo Against Knowing Who You Are](http://www.amazon.com/Book-Taboo-Against-Knowing-Who/dp/0679723005/sr=1-1/qid=1170207477/ref=pd_bbs_sr_1/105-6244180-7165267?ie=UTF8&s=books) by Alan Watts

[![The Book: On the Taboo Against Knowing Who You Are](http://ec2.images-amazon.com/images/P/0679723005.01._BO2,204,203,200_PIsitb-dp-500-arrow,TopRight,45,-64_OU01_AA240_SH20_SCLZZZZZZZ_.jpg)](http://www.amazon.com/gp/reader/0679723005/ref=sib_dp_pt/105-6244180-7165267#reader-link)

There are many wonderful perspectives that he presents in this book.  It is a book that I plan to give to my son when he is in high school.

[Spontaneous Awakening](http://www.amazon.com/Spontaneous-Awakening-Adyashanti/dp/1591792916/sr=1-1/qid=1170208053/ref=pd_bbs_sr_1/105-6244180-7165267?ie=UTF8&s=books) by Adyashanti

[![Spontaneous Awakening](http://ec1.images-amazon.com/images/P/1591792916.01._AA240_SCLZZZZZZZ_.jpg)](http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/images/1591792916/ref=dp_image_0/105-6244180-7165267?ie=UTF8&n=283155&s=books)

This was a very powerful listen.  Adya is a wonderful speaker, he could be your next door neighbor.  His insights are much the same as Alan Watts and conveyed in a less intellectual way.  They, Alan and Adya, make for an effective environment to awaken.
